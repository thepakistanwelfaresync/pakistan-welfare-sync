About the Founder

Meet the visionary behind The Pakistan Welfare Sync

Immad Mansoor

Founder & Visionary Leader

Immad Mansoor is the Founder of The Pakistan Welfare Sync and a dynamic undergraduate scholar pursuing his BS in Computer Science at Air University. Driven by the belief that real leadership is measured by the ability to build systems that outlast the work itself, he established this organization to bridge the gap between advanced technology and grassroots social welfare.

With over 4 years of professional experience in video editing, graphic design, and digital strategy, Immad brings a unique industry perspective to the nonprofit sector. His professional background includes working with multiple international brands such as ICON.

On campus, Immad is a recognized student leader, serving as the Class Representative (CR) for two distinct cohorts and the official Graphic Designer for the AUCIS Society (Air University Computing and Innovation Society). Combining his technical skillset ranging from C++ programming and prompt engineering to digital storytelling with a passion for community service, Immad's vision is to move beyond traditional charity. He aims to create sustainable, data-driven educational and social support systems that empower the youth and underserved communities of Pakistan.

Core Tech Initiatives

Tech-Link Welfare: Bridging technology and social impact through innovative digital solutions

Geospatial Mapping

Creating custom Indoor Digital Navigation Layers for complex buildings (University and Hospital departments) using Google My Maps to solve navigation problems and improve accessibility.

Data-Driven Counseling

Collecting 'Real-World Data' on career trends and visualizing it into statistical infographics to guide students, replacing guesswork with evidence-based decision making.

Digital Media Education

Bridging the digital divide by training non-tech sectors (Medical Students, School Students) in video editing and content creation for social causes and professional growth.

The 4-Phase Operational Model

A systematic, scalable approach to creating sustainable digital infrastructure across Pakistan

01

University

Foundation & Mapping

Establish partnerships with universities, create digital navigation systems, and build the foundational infrastructure.

02

Hospital

Medical Wing & Skill Transfer

Extend mapping to hospitals, train medical students in digital media, and create health communication channels.

03

Educational Outreach

Data-Driven Counseling

Collect career data, create visual analytics, and provide evidence-based guidance to students across schools.

04

Social Welfare

Impact Finale & Distribution

Deploy all digital systems at scale, measure impact, and ensure sustainable operation for long-term community benefit.
